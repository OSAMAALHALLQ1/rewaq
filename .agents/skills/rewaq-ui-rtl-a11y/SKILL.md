# RTL and UX Review

1. Validate Arabic RTL alignment, logical spacing, icon direction, numeric readability, currency, and dates.
2. Test desktop, tablet, and mobile layouts.
3. Ensure forms show clear labels, validation, disabled/loading state, and recoverable errors.
4. Tables need pagination/virtualization for large data, filters that truly affect queries, and accessible keyboard behavior.
5. No button, export control, selector, or filter may be cosmetic.
6. Hide developer jargon, environment variable names, and infrastructure details from restaurant users.
7. Use role-appropriate terminology: cashier, waiter, kitchen station, warehouse, branch, supplier.
8. Ensure print layouts for receipts, invoices, purchase orders, stock counts, and reports.
9. Check color contrast, focus indicators, semantics, labels, and screen-reader names.
10. Provide findings ordered by severity and exact component/route.
